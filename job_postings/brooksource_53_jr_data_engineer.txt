Job Description
Junior Data Engineer
Location: Downtown Cincinnati
A junior data engineer is an entry-level data professional (0-18 months of experience) responsible for designing, building,
and maintaining data pipelines and systems. They work closely with Product Owners, data analysts, and business
stakeholders to ensure data is accessible and high-quality. Junior data engineers typically have less experience than data
engineers and are expected to work under the guidance of experienced engineers.
Job Summary
We are seeking a highly motivated, detail-oriented Entry-Level Data Engineer to join our dynamic team. The successful
candidate will assist in gathering requirements, curating data elements and documenting data lineage in specified data
repositories within the bank. This entry-level position offers an excellent opportunity to gain hands-on experience in data
engineering and to work closely with experienced professionals.
Duties and Responsibilities
· Curating data elements and documenting data lineage
· Managing data storage and ensuring data quality

· Building and maintaining data pipelines and ETL processes – moving data between different cloud-based platforms and on-
premises data sources

· Working with Product Owners and Analysts to understand business requirements and provide data solutions
· Writing scripts and code to automate data processes
· Develop and maintain data models and transformation logic using DBT in a cloud or on-prem data environment.
· Participate in Agile ceremonies and contribute to sprint planning and reviews.
Requirements and Qualifications
· Strong problem-solving and communication skills
· Proficiency in at least one programming language (Python, Java, etc.)
· In-depth understanding of commonly used Databases, Data Warehousing concepts, and ETL technologies (DataStage, dbt)
· High level of proficiency using SQL
· Strong understanding of joining data in SQL
· Understanding of big data technologies (Curation, Data Lineage, Data Mesh, etc.)
· Experience with cloud platforms (Snowflake, AWS etc.)
