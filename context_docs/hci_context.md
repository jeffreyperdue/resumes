# 1. Opportunity Evaluation & Strategic Framing

## Core Concepts Applied

- Defined structured problem/solution statements for consistent product positioning.
- Evaluated ideas using the Desirability–Feasibility–Viability framework.
- Applied Impact Mapping:
  - Goal → Actors → Impacts → Deliverables.
- Used Impact/Effort matrices for feature prioritization.
- Conducted SWOT and PESTEL analysis.
- Evaluated competitive positioning and differentiation.
- Assessed risks related to feasibility, market entry, and adoption.

## Resume-Relevant Signals

- Strategic product thinking
- Business-model awareness
- Feature prioritization discipline
- Structured decision-making
- Cross-functional alignment

# 2. Market Analysis & Segmentation

## Core Concepts Applied

- Estimated market size using:
  - TAM (Total Addressable Market)
  - SAM (Serviceable Available Market)
  - SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)
- Applied top-down, bottom-up, and value-theory estimation approaches.
- Identified early adopters for initial traction validation.
- Used ATAR model:
  - Awareness
  - Trial
  - Availability
  - Repeat
- Analyzed risks related to “Crossing the Chasm” (early adopters → early majority).

## Resume-Relevant Signals

- Market sizing experience
- Adoption modeling
- Product-market fit validation
- Go-to-market awareness
- Business viability analysis

## Core Concepts Applied

- Designed and conducted:

- Ease of use

Methods used:

Identified UI improvements aligned with mental models and usability standards.

# 5. Mental Models, Cognitive Load & Interaction Design

## Core Concepts Applied

- Designed interfaces aligned with user mental models.
- Reduced:
  - Gulf of execution
  - Gulf of evaluation
- Distinguished between:
  - Slips (execution errors)
  - Mistakes (goal/intention errors)
- Reduced cognitive load through simplified task flows.
- Improved:
  - Visibility
  - Feedback mechanisms
  - Error prevention
  - Recovery (Undo, Exit, Pause patterns)

## Resume-Relevant Signals

- Behavioral design thinking
- Error-reduction strategy
- Flow optimization
- Cognitive clarity in interaction design

# 7. Experimentation & A/B Testing

## Core Concepts Applied

Structured A/B testing process:

1. Identify conversion goal
2. Define hypothesis
3. Design controlled variations
4. Randomize users
5. Measure outcomes
6. Analyze results
7. Iterate

Understood tradeoffs between speed of testing and depth of insight.

## Resume-Relevant Signals

- Experiment design
- Conversion optimization
- Hypothesis-driven development
- Growth-oriented product thinking
